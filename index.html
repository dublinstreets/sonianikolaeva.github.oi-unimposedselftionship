Over the winter 2020 and until further notice, the World was hit by self–isolation rules and social distancing measures caused by the health crisis. For many people across the World, their own Self became the only available ‘Someone’ to talk to, to move with, to stay entertained and happy. The whole World has to improvise in the current circumstances, so have to do people in their everyday routines. In The Netherlands, we haven’t been locked down, thus going outside became a privilege that is approached in new and uncommon ways. People discover new corners of their mental states, often dark and sad ones—depressing notes are often present in people these days and the way to deal with it isn’t exactly known. For me, as for many many other people, going out became an elixir for life. My main action was photographic improvisation via which I tried to capture my different mental states and reflect on them as something recorded and documented. I observed, moved, stayed still, listened in silence, smelled, touched and went through opposite emotions as being under the dark sky or hot sun. The experiences and memories stay with me — they don’t pass by. All done alone, from waking up to taking a photograph on a set timer. These are weird and vague times. We are in a not alone loneliness as long as each of us is in a friendship with the Self — in the Selftionship.
The going out impovisations consist of 5 chapter -- every of them is showcasing a specific way of interaction with the environment and a specific way of looking at it.
